  • 10/2016 - 05/2018
    Duke University Department of Pharmacology and Cancer Biology
    Research Lab Assistant at the Counter Lab
    When I first started my college career at Duke, I wanted to study Biomedical Engineering and pursue a career track in research. I worked under a PhD candidate in Dr. Christopher Counter's lab, primarily doing DNA sequencing via PCR, scanning mice thyroid tissue for tumors and other irregularities, and overall learning lab etiquette and procedure.

    Our research primarily focused on analyzing the effect of copper chelation on papillary thyroid cancers with an oncogenic BRAF mutation, which is present in 60% of all cases. This led to a paper later published in Clinical Cancer Research at the American Association for Cancer Research.

  • 06/2015 - 07/2015
    Rice University Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering
    Research Lab Intern at the Li Lab
    As part of the Rice University Office of STEM Engagement's Research Experience for High School Students (REHSS) program, I spent the summer after my junior year of high school interning for Dr. Qilin Li's research lab. This was my first experience being exposed to research, and ultimately influenced me to initially pursue biomedical engineering when I got to Duke.

    Our research primarily focused on the effect of d-tyrosine on biofilm formation. These biofilm growth and accumulation particularly pose problems in reverse osmosis and nanofiltration technologies, which are important for water treatment plants. During this summer, I cultured bacterial colonies and conducted experiments to determine ideal d-tyrosine concentrations to reduce biofilm formation. At the end of the program, each intern wrote papers on their research for the summer.
